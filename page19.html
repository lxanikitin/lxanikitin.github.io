Virus threat: Bitcoin Address Switcher

Have you ever come across the situation of copying your wallet’s BTC address but pasting a slightly different one?
Beware, it is a clear sign that your device is infected with a Trojan virus. According to Symantec databases Trojan.Coinbitclip is a malignant software that replaces several digits in the address you saved to the clipboard with other digits supplied by the virus.

How does it work?
The Trojan horse is triggered when certain digits similar to the Bitcoin address are copied. The virus uses a custom database of numerous third-party Bitcoin addresses to replace them with the recently copied address immediately after detection. If you are not sure this is a virus, you can check yourself by copying and pasting some text or a number not similar to BTC address. If, in this case, the copy/paste goes correctly, but the pasted BTC address is different from the copied one, then your device is affected by the Trojan.

What should I do?
If you have already sent BTC to an incorrect address, unfortunately, we cannot do anything now. BTC transactions are irreversible by its nature.

To prevent this situation we strongly recommend you to follow our advice:

Please read the official manual for removing the virus provided by Symantec;
Undergo a full scan of your device for malware;
Make sure your device is equipped with the latest version of anti-virus;
Regularly run full scans with your anti-virus software;
Secure your network
Keep your personal information safe;
Do not click on untrustworthy links;
Avoid suspicious sites and unlicensed software;
Be careful next time.

Note: eCoinSupply is not involved in this activity in any way and hence not responsible for any loss.

I haven’t received my email confirmation letter. What do I do?

If you haven't received your email confirmation letter, the two most likely reasons are that either your email client has put the email in your spam folder or you gave us the wrong address when signing up. Here's a little troubleshooting guide to help you pinpoint the problem:

Check your spam folder for an email titled "Welcome to eCoinSupply."

If the email isn't in your spam folder, you need to determine whether you entered the right address or not. You can do this by trying to make a new account with the email you used to create your current account. If you’ve used this address before, the system will let you know that it’s already been used to create an account, and won't let you make another one. If it does let you create a new account, then you entered the wrong email when registering your first account, and you will receive an email confirmation letter for the new account.

If the attempt to create the second account confirms that the first one has the correct email yet you still can't find the confirmation letter for it, then try waiting awhile before checking your email again. Sometimes it just takes some time for the email confirmation letter to arrive.

If the letter hasn't arrived after two hours, contact our customer support.

Problems logging in

If you can't log into your account, this probably means you're entering the wrong
username, password, or two-factor authentication code (one-time password).

If an email address or/and a password you are entering differ from those used to sign up,
you will get an error "Invalid credentials".

Here is what you need to do:

To reset your password, go to Sign in > "Forgot your password?" and enter the email
address you used to sign up to eCoinSupply. Check your email for the password reset link.

At this stage, an error "Reset password token is invalid" may occur.

This can happen if you have sent the password reset email several times - please make sure
that you follow the password reset link in the last received email. If the problem
persists, please, clear your browser cache and cookies.

Please note, reset password link is active for 6 hours.

The login problem may have been caused by your two-factor authentication getting out
of sync. In this case please contact our support team: they will assist you with resetting
your two-factor authentication token. After you regain access to your account, we
recommend you delete your old two-factor authentication and create a new one.

Is it possible to turn off the new device ID confirmation?

Yes, it is possible but for your security, we need to make sure you have the 2FA enabled for your account. To enable it please go to the Settings page and find the Security tab. Once you turn on the 2FA let us know and we'll disable the new device ID confirmation for your account.

How do I confirm a new device ID?

A device ID (device identification) is a distinctive number associated with a laptop, smartphone or a similar electronic device. As an additional security measure, eCoinSupply customers are now required to confirm every new device they use. It means that when you log in from another PC, laptop, tablet or phone or use browser's incognito or private browsing modes you will be shown an Authorize device screen and an email will be sent to your mailbox. You will need to click the confirmation button to get to your account.

If you haven't received a confirmation email after 20 minutes, please try resending it using a Resend confirmation email button or check your Spam folder. 

How do I reset my 2FA?

To reset your 2FA, please contact our support team.

We would require you to answer a couple of questions regarding your account to prove your ownership.

If everything is correct - we will turn the 2FA off so that you can easily log in to your account. Don't forget to turn the 2FA back on, once you've successfully logged in. 

What is multisig?

Multi-signature transactions can exist on the Bitcoin network; they basically require multiple private keys in order to sign a transaction. This means multiple parties must agree on an outcome before funds are released, and provides extra security.

New Device Confirmation email doesn't work!

If you haven’t received your New Device Confirmation email please do the following:

Check your Spam box/Junk mail.
Try clearing your cache.

If you have received your New Device Confirmation email, but the button doesn’t work or you’re redirected to the same page please do the following:

Try using a different browser and send your device confirmation letter one more time by clicking "resend confirmation email"

If you’re redirected to the same page after you’ve pressed “Confirm New Device” button please try refreshing the page.

If nothing mentioned above has solved your problem please feel free to contact our support team and report on the issue.

What is 2FA and how to turn it on?

Two-Factor Authentication, also known as 2FA, TFA, two-step verification or multi-factor authentication, is an extra layer of security that requires not only a login and password but also something that only an account holder knows and has an access to. In our case, we require your mobile phone with a special App where a single-use code is generated. Once 2FA is enabled, you will need to enter this six-digit code to the correspondent field every time you log in.

This adds a second layer of defence and makes it almost impossible for anyone but you to access your account.
