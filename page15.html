Getting verified

What is eCoinSupply verification?

eCoinSupply verification process is divided into a few steps: depending on how you are going to use the account, we offer you 3 tiers of verification.

Higher tiers mean that more options become available for you, the deposit/withdrawal limits are raised, and the protection of your account is reinforced. In order to upgrade a tier, you are asked to provide an additional document which confirms your identity. These verification requirements, also known as KYC (Know Your Customer), help us enhance the security of the service and the safety of our clients’ funds.

You may choose the verification tier which fits your needs based on the limits and services you require.

Tier 1 – you can use our wallet to store, exchange, send and receive BTC/LTC/XRP/ETH (digital currencies). We also offer accounts in USD, EUR and GBP which can be used to exchange currencies and thus avoid the market fluctuations. You can learn more about how eCoinSupply wallet works while reading the corresponding article in our Helpdesk. To get to Tier 1 you need to verify your phone number.

Tier 2 – you can make a SEPA deposit in EUR to your eCoinSupply account and buy BTC, LTC, XRP or ETH with your card if it supports 3D secure.

Also, you can withdraw funds to your bank account. More detailed information can be found in the Helpdesk section on Deposits and withdrawals. In order to obtain Tier 2, you are required to submit a proof of ID and a selfie with your ID in your hands.

Tier 3 – enjoy bank transfers and deposits with the card with much higher limits than at Tier 2. You will be required to upload a proof of address at this stage.

*Total fiat deposits and withdrawals on the Tier 2 are limited to 15 000 EUR per annum

What documents do I submit to verify my account?

In order to verify your account, you will need to submit 3 types of documents:

1. A valid government-issued ID. High-quality images of the following documents are acceptable:

Passport (double page)
National ID card (both sides)
Driving license (both sides) 
Important: To avoid delays when verifying your account, make sure that your document is:

Entirely visible (watermarks are permitted, but they must not cover any major details);
High-quality (colour images with resolution of at least 300 DPI and more than 100 Kb in size);
Valid (its expiry date must be clearly visible and not due in the next 6 months).

2. A proof of residency. High-quality scans of the following documents are acceptable:

Bank statement of your current account;
Credit card statement;
Utility bills: water, electricity, etc.
Broadband home internet bill, landline phone bill, bundles, etc.
Tax return, council tax bill;
Government-issued certificate of residency.

Important: To ensure that your verification goes smooth and fast. 

take a photo of a paper document (with your mobile phone) or upload an original digital version of it in PDF;
make sure, it has been issued in the last 3 months;
it’s addressed to your name and home address.

Note: To avoid delays make sure the documents you provide are NOT protected by a password. If you can't remove the password (for example, the password is set by a bank) please contact our support team after uploading your document.

3. A selfie with your ID document in your hands.
Please, make sure that every detail of the ID document is clearly visible.

Note: Screenshots, mobile phone bills, medical bills, and insurance documents are NOT accepted for verification. For documents drafted in languages that use non-Latin characters (Arabic, Chinese, etc.), a certified translation into English is required. 

How long does verification take?

Usually it doesn’t take eCoinSupply moe than a couple of days to process verification applications. However, if we experience a heavy influx of new users, processing times for verification can slow down considerably. If you experience delays it doesn't necessarily mean there is something wrong with your application.

Please be patient, and we'll contact you if we need any further information from you. Keep an eye on the email inbox associated with your eCoinSupplyaccount to see if you've received a letter from us regarding your verification.

Why does the file size need to be at least 100 kB? 

Although we’ve done our best to make verification quick and easy, there still can be some difficulties with file size and image resolution

When uploading a file for verification, sometimes you get this error: “the file is too small”. These size and resolution requirements have been adopted due to security reasons. In order to make sure that the photo of a document has been made by the owner and not simply copied or stolen, the file needs to be at least 100 kB in size.

Normally, a phone camera takes pictures that range in file size from 1.5 MB to 2.5 MB. So what you would want to do is to make sure that you are not making your file smaller accidentally.

1. How do I learn the properties of my photo?

Mac OS: right-click on the file and choose “Get info”.

Windows: its size will be shown in your Windows Explorer bar when you click on the file.

Android: you can choose the size of pictures yourself in settings.

iPhone: unfortunately, iPhone doesn’t give users an opportunity to find out the image size but you don’t need to worry, because all iPhone-taken photos are bigger than 100 kB.

2. What if you’ve taken a picture with a 13-megapixel camera and you still see this error when uploading?

This must be due to the import method you've used. If you send your picture using a messenger or via email, it automatically compresses and makes it a few times smaller.

Please note: web cameras and laptop built-in cameras take pictures of the small size and poor quality, and are not advisable for taking pictures that you plan to use for verification.

3. What can I do then?

If you upload verification documents from your computer, import photos using the standard method that is suggested when you connect your device to a computer, i.e. Autoplay import in Windows and iPhoto in macOS. In this case you can be 100% sure that the image remains as it is: without any conversion or resizing.

Alternatively, if you take photos with an iPhone, it is much easier to upload them using eCoinSupply application.

To start verification go to “Other” -> “Settings” -> “Start verification”.

eCoinSupply app takes and uploads pictures simultaneously, so you don’t have to worry about size and resolution. 

Verification tips by country 

Verification tips for UK residents

Here we present the list of documents, which you can use to confirm your identity and residential address. Note that the list is not exhaustive, but only gives samples of documents and companies/establishments, which can provide you with necessary documents.

1. For identity verification you can submit your valid UK driving license or UK passport. In exceptional cases, when a customer doesn’t possess any of these two documents, he/she may provide Proof of Age card as an ID. Detailed information about the card can be found via this link https://www.validateuk.co.uk/

2. For address verification, you may provide one of the documents from the list below. Make sure it shows your name, home address and was issued no earlier than 3 months ago. We accept both photos and scans as well as digital PDF versions of your address documents. In most cases original electronic documents (bills, bank statements) in PDF, provided by your utility company/internet provider/bank are preferable (if available).

3. Utility bills from the following companies are accepted (including, but not limited to): British Gas, Extra Energy, Ovoenergy, Npower, Thames Water, PFP Energy, Sainsbury’s Energy, First Utility, Utility Warehouse, EDF Energy, Anglian water, Good Energy, SSE Swalec, SSE Southern Electric, Green Star Energy, SPARK, Scottish Power, Affinity Water, Severn Trent, Bristol Energy, Iresa Limited, GB Energy Supply etc.

4. Internet and landline phone bills from: EE, Hyperoptic, SKY, Tesco, BT, Virgin Media etc.

5. Current/savings account statements and credit card statements from the following banks and building societies, regulated by the FCA: Barclays Bank, HSBC, Lloyds Bank, Santander Bank, Tesco Bank, Bank of Scotland, Starling Bank, The Royal Bank of Scotland, Halifax, NatWest Bank, Ulster Bank, TSB Bank, The co-operative bank, Nationwide building society

6. Council Tax Bills, issued within last 3 months as at uploading date

7. We also accept tax documents and notifications from HM Revenue & Customs

We DO NOT accept mobile phone bills, insurance documents, medical bills, mortgage statements, different loan/credit documents, statements from e-money services and traffic/parking fines as a proof of address. 

Verification tips for residents of Japan

The major issue our clients from Japan face while trying to get verified is the documents' language - we do not accept documents with non-latin characters, so documents in Japanese cannot be accepted.

A customer has two options - the document must either be translated into English by a certified translation agency or it must be drafted in English right from the start.

For the confirmation of identity we accept the Japanese passport - all details there are written in English and you don't have to translate it. Other ID documents, such as a driver's licence, must be translated into English.

The easiest way to verify your address is to provide a bank statement in English. We accept some current account statements from such banks as SMBC, Resona Bank or Sumito Mitsui Banking Corporation. Those major banks do provide statements in English. All other documents must be coupled with a certified translation into English. 

Verification tips for residents of Germany

1. Proof of identity - ID card (Ausweis) (both sides), passport (Reisepass) or driving licence (Führerschein) (both sides).

2. To verify your address, you can submit the following types of documents: bank statements, utility bills, Internet bills and landline phone bills and Meldebestätigungen (issued by your local municipality).

3. The list of banks, which provide necessary documents (including, but not limited to): Deutsche bank, N26, Sparkassen, ING Bank, Commerzbank, Fidor, Deutsche Kreditbank, Postbank, Comdirect. NOTE: Paper statements are accepted only with a bank seal, put in a bank branch!! E-statements are accepted only if original PDF file is provided.

4. Major Internet providers: 1&1, Telekom Deutschland, Unity Media, UPC etc. E-Rechnungen are preferable. In case you submit a paper bill you might be requested to submit an additional address document.

5. We do accept Einkommersteuerbescheide, but they must at least have some QR-codes or bar codes.

6. We do NOT accept different payment stubs from employers because the majority of such documents do not have any logo, seal or stamp or QR (bar) codes. 

Verification tips for residents of Brazil

1. You may submit your RG card for identity verification. However, make sure you provide a valid RG card because if the individual is under 16 at the time he/she applies for the card, it will expire on his/her 18th birthday, making it necessary to apply for another one. Thus, if you submit such RG card, it won't be accepted.

2. We accept the following documents as a proof of residence (including, but not limited to): bills from Brazilian electricity suppliers - AES Eletropaulo, Coelba, Copel, CPFL, RGE, Energisa; internet providers - NET, Vivo, Oi; banks - Itau Bank, Hipercard etc.

3. The following tax documents are accepted as e-documents only (PDF) - Relatório de Situação Fiscal and RECIBO DE ENTREGA DA DECLARAÇÃO DE AJUSTE ANUAL.

4. In case a person doesn't have any documents, confirming his/her residential address or shares an accommodation, there is another option - DECLARAÇÃO DE RESIDENCIA, a document, signed by a landlord or property owner, confirming that a customer resides at the specific address.
